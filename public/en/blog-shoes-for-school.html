How to Choose School Shoes: The Complete Guide for Parents

August 15, 20245 minute read

The beginning of the school year comes with a long to-do list, and choosing appropriate shoes for children is one of the most important tasks. The right shoes not only contribute to the child's daily comfort, but also affect foot development, posture and overall health. In this guide, we'll go over everything you need to know to choose the perfect school shoes.

Why is it important to choose the right shoes?

Children's feet are in a constant process of development, and inappropriate shoes can cause long-term problems. Shoes that are too tight can distort the shape of the foot and cause pain, while shoes that are too wide don't provide adequate support and can lead to falls and injuries. Additionally, children spend many hours at school, walking, running and playing, so shoes need to be comfortable and supportive throughout the day.

Studies show that many children wear shoes in the wrong size, which can affect their walking pattern and cause back and knee pain. Proper shoe selection is an investment in the child's long-term health.

How to properly measure the foot?

Proper measurement is the foundation for successful selection. It's recommended to measure your child's foot at the end of the day, when feet are slightly more swollen - this way you'll get a more accurate picture of the required size. The child should stand upright during measurement, as the foot expands slightly under body weight.

It's important to measure both feet, as there's sometimes a size difference between them. In such cases, always choose according to the larger foot. Leave a centimeter gap between the toe tips and the shoe's end - this space is essential for comfort and allows the foot to move naturally during walking.

What to look for in a quality shoe?

A good school shoe must meet several important criteria. First, the material should be breathable - natural leather or quality fabrics allow feet to breathe and prevent excessive sweating. The sole should be flexible yet supportive, with good shock absorption to protect joints during running and playing.

Pay attention to the back part of the shoe - it should be stable and support the heel without pressing. A good closure system, whether laces or velcro, is important to ensure the shoe stays stable on the foot. Shoes with laces allow for more precise adjustment, but for younger children, velcro might be more convenient for independent use.

Common mistakes to avoid

One of the most common mistakes is buying shoes in a size too large 'so they'll last longer.' Although this seems economical, shoes that are too big can cause trips and injuries, and don't provide the required support. On the other hand, shoes that are too tight will cause discomfort and may harm foot development.

Another mistake is ignoring the child's type of activity. A child who plays a lot of soccer during breaks will need shoes with support and quality laces, while a child who spends most time in the classroom can make do with lighter shoes. Don't forget to replace shoes every 3-4 months or when they become too small - children's feet grow quickly.

Practical tips for smart shopping

Take your child with you to the store and let them try on and walk in the shoes before purchasing. A child who feels comfortable in the shoes will be more willing to wear them daily. Check that the shoe doesn't press at any point and that there's enough room for toes to move freely.

If possible, prefer to buy two pairs of shoes and alternate between them - this allows the shoes to air out and extends their lifespan. Additionally, pay attention to school regulations regarding shoe color and type, to avoid the need for later replacement. Proper selection of school shoes is an investment in the child's comfort, health, and confidence throughout the school year.
